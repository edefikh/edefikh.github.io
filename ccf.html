✅ Common Control Framework (CCF) Implementation Checklist

1. Planning & Preparation

  • ✔ Define Scope – Identify regulations, frameworks, and industry standards relevant to your organization (e.g., NIST CSF, ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI DSS, HIPAA, GDPR).
  • ✔ Assemble a CCF Team – Include compliance officers, security professionals, legal advisors, and business stakeholders.
  • ✔ Identify Business Objectives – Align controls with business goals and risk appetite.
  • ✔ Conduct a Gap Analysis – Compare existing controls against required frameworks.

2. Develop the CCF

  • ✔ Map Frameworks & Requirements – Identify overlapping controls across multiple regulations.
  • ✔ Create a Unified Control Set – Define control categories (e.g., access control, data protection, incident response).
  • ✔ Assign Control Owners – Designate responsibility for each control’s implementation and monitoring.
  • ✔ Document Policies & Procedures – Establish clear guidelines for how controls are implemented.

3. Implementation & Integration

  • ✔ Automate Where Possible – Use GRC tools (e.g., OneTrust, ServiceNow, RSA Archer) to track compliance.
  • ✔ Integrate with Existing Processes – Ensure CCF aligns with IT, security, and business workflows.
  • ✔ Provide Employee Training – Educate staff on new policies and controls.
  • ✔ Implement Continuous Monitoring – Set up dashboards and alerts for control deviations.

4. Assessment & Validation

  • ✔ Conduct Internal Audits – Regularly assess the effectiveness of controls.
  • ✔ Perform Risk Assessments – Identify potential weaknesses and update controls accordingly.
  • ✔ Prepare for External Audits – Ensure documentation is audit-ready for regulatory assessments.

5. Maintenance & Continuous Improvement

  • ✔ Monitor Regulatory Changes – Stay updated on compliance changes and adjust controls accordingly.
  • ✔ Review & Update the CCF – Regularly refine controls based on business needs and evolving threats.
  • ✔ Gather Feedback – Use lessons learned to enhance the framework.
  • ✔ Ensure Executive Buy-in – Keep leadership engaged in governance and risk management.
