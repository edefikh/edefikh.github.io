Difference Between SOC 2 and ISO 27001

1. Purpose and Focus

SOC 2: A framework developed by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) that focuses on the security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy of customer data. It's specifically designed for technology and cloud computing companies that provide services to other businesses.

ISO 27001: An international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS). It provides a risk-based approach to security management applicable to organizations of all types.

2. Certification vs. Attestation

SOC 2: Provides an attestation report issued by a CPA firm, assessing whether an organization's security controls meet the required Trust Service Criteria.

ISO 27001: Offers a formal certification issued by accredited bodies, proving compliance with the standard.

3. Geographic and Industry Adoption

SOC 2: Primarily used in North America, especially for cloud-based service providers and SaaS companies.

ISO 27001: More widely recognized globally, applicable across all industries, including finance, healthcare, and manufacturing.

4. Scope and Structure

SOC 2: Focuses on controls relevant to customer data protection, specifically tailored to the service organization's commitments.

ISO 27001: Covers a broader set of security controls across the entire organization, including risk assessment, governance, policies, and continuous improvement.

5. Audit and Assessment

SOC 2: Two types of reports:

  • Type I: A point-in-time assessment of security controls.
  • Type II: An evaluation over a period (typically 3-12 months) to assess operational effectiveness.

ISO 27001: Requires an initial certification audit and ongoing surveillance audits (typically annual) to maintain compliance.

When to Choose SOC 2 vs. ISO 27001?

✅ Choose SOC 2 if:

  • You’re a cloud service provider or SaaS company selling to U.S.-based businesses.
  • Your customers request SOC 2 reports as proof of security compliance.
  • You need to demonstrate security but don’t necessarily require an ISO certification.
  • You prefer a framework that evaluates customer-facing security controls.

✅ Choose ISO 27001 if:

  • You operate internationally or work with European and global businesses.
  • You need a structured and comprehensive security management system beyond IT controls.
  • You want a recognized security certification that applies across industries.
  • You’re in a regulated industry that requires a more standardized approach.

Can You Have Both?

Yes! Many companies pursue both SOC 2 and ISO 27001 to cover different compliance needs. For example, a U.S.-based SaaS company expanding internationally might first obtain SOC 2 to meet U.S. client expectations and then pursue ISO 27001 for global credibility.
