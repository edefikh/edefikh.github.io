CMMC v2.0 Implementation Timeline

This timeline provides a structured approach for small businesses to achieve CMMC v2.0 compliance within 12 months.

Month-by-Month Breakdown

  • Month 1-2:
    • Determine required CMMC Level (1, 2, or 3) based on contracts and DoD requirements.
    • Identify Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) within the business.
    • Perform a gap analysis against NIST 800-171 and CMMC requirements.
    • Assign a compliance lead and establish a governance structure.
  • Month 3-4:
    • Develop a System Security Plan (SSP).
    • Create a Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M) to address deficiencies.
    • Implement foundational security controls (multi-factor authentication, access controls, encryption).
    • Begin staff security awareness training.
  • Month 5-6:
    • Implement incident response and monitoring procedures.
    • Establish continuous monitoring and logging mechanisms.
    • Secure cloud environments and third-party vendor risk assessments.
    • Conduct an internal risk assessment and refine the POA&M.
  • Month 7-8:
    • Test security controls for effectiveness.
    • Conduct internal audits and document findings.
    • Review and update security policies and procedures.
    • Ensure compliance with CMMC reporting requirements.
  • Month 9-10:
    • Conduct a final self-assessment based on NIST 800-171A.
    • Remediate any remaining gaps identified in the assessment.
    • Schedule and prepare for a third-party CMMC assessment (if required).
  • Month 11-12:
    • Engage with a C3PAO (CMMC Third-Party Assessor Organization) for certification.
    • Conduct a formal CMMC assessment.
    • Address any remaining corrective actions as required.
    • Maintain ongoing compliance through continuous monitoring and updates.

Post-Certification

After achieving CMMC certification, businesses must maintain compliance by continuously monitoring security controls, updating policies, and preparing for periodic reassessments.
