ISO 27001 Audit Assessment Questions

1. General Understanding of ISO 27001 Efforts

  • Have you attempted ISO 27001 certification before? If so, what were the results?
  • What are the key challenges preventing successful certification?
  • Do you have an Information Security Management System (ISMS) in place?
  • Who is responsible for maintaining and improving the ISMS?
  • What is your leadership’s commitment to ISO 27001 compliance?

2. Governance & Documentation

  • Do you have an ISO 27001 policy framework (e.g., Information Security Policy, Risk Management Policy, Access Control Policy, etc.)?
  • When was the last time policies were reviewed and approved?
  • How do you communicate security policies to employees?
  • Do you have an asset inventory of all critical IT systems and data?
  • What security training and awareness programs are in place?

3. Risk Management & Internal Audits

  • Have you conducted a formal risk assessment based on ISO 27001 requirements?
  • How do you identify and mitigate security risks?
  • Do you have a documented risk treatment plan?
  • When was the last time an internal audit was conducted?
  • Have previous internal audits identified any major non-conformities? If so, how were they handled?
  • How do you track corrective and preventive actions?

4. Technical & Operational Security Controls

  • Do you follow Annex A controls (A.5–A.18) in ISO/IEC 27001:2022?
  • How are access controls managed (e.g., role-based access, least privilege)?
  • Are security configurations regularly reviewed and updated?
  • What is your approach to vulnerability management and patching?
  • How is data encryption handled (at rest and in transit)?
  • Do you conduct security monitoring and incident detection?
  • Do you have documented incident response and disaster recovery plans?

5. Compliance & Third-Party Security

  • Do you have a process for ensuring compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements?
  • How do you manage third-party risk (vendors, suppliers, cloud providers)?
  • Are vendors required to meet specific security requirements (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, NIST CSF)?
  • Have you performed supplier audits or risk assessments?

6. Audit History & Lessons Learned

  • What findings did the last ISO 27001 audit uncover?
  • Were the issues related to documentation, implementation, or monitoring?
  • How did you address past audit non-conformities?
  • Have you engaged external consultants or experts to assist with ISO 27001 compliance?
  • What support or resources do you need to improve compliance?

Next Steps

After gathering this information, prioritize the most critical gaps and risks and create a roadmap for remediation.
